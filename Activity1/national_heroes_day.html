Description

National Heroes' Day (Araw ng mga Bayani) in the Philippines is commemorated every last Monday of August. This significant holiday pays tribute to the countless known and unknown Filipinos who fought for the nation's freedom and helped shape its identity throughout history.

The observance dates back to 1931 when the Philippine Legislature first designated August 30 as National Heroes' Day. This date was chosen to commemorate the 1896 "Cry of Pugad Lawin" (Sigaw ng Pugad Lawin), marking the beginning of the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonial rule under the leadership of Andrés Bonifacio. In 2007, the date was adjusted to the last Monday of August to create a longer weekend for citizens.

  • Jose Rizal: National hero whose writings sparked revolutionary sentiment
  • Andres Bonifacio: Founder of the Katipunan revolutionary society
  • Gabriela Silang: Female revolutionary leader who fought against Spanish Colonization
  • Lapu-Lapu: Chieftain who defeated Ferdinand Magelland's Forces in 1521
