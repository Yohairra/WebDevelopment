Description

All Saints' Day, known as "Undas" in the Philippines, is a deeply significant holiday observed every November 1st. It is a day dedicated to honoring all the saints of the Catholic Church, but in the Philippines, it has also become a time for remembering and paying respects to departed loved ones.

On this day, Filipino families visit cemeteries to clean and decorate the graves of their deceased relatives with flowers, candles, and prayers. Many also bring food and spend hours—sometimes even the whole day—at the cemetery, reminiscing about their loved ones. This practice strengthens family bonds and upholds the Filipino value of maintaining close ties even beyond death.

Churches across the country hold special masses for the souls of the departed. People offer prayers and light candles as a sign of remembrance. In some provinces, traditional rituals, songs, and food offerings still play a part in the observance.
